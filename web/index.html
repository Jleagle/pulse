Privacy Policy

At Pulse, protecting your personal health data is our foundational design principle. We built Pulse using a 100% stateless backend server architecture so that your sensitive health metrics remain strictly under your control, are never stored in backend databases, and are never shared with advertisers or third parties.

1. 100% Stateless Backend & Transient Server Processing

Pulse operates without database servers, persistent storage volumes, or data lakes. When you connect your Google Account, our backend Go server processes data requests purely in volatile system memory during an active API call to format metrics for your dashboard. Once your HTTP request completes, server-side memory buffers are released.

2. Local Browser Caching (6-Hour Expiry)

To ensure instantaneous dashboard navigation and prevent Google Health API rate-limiting, Pulse stores fetched metrics locally on your device within your web browser's localStorage (key: pulse_app_cache). This local cache automatically expires after 6 hours. You can instantly purge your local browser cache at any time via the Clear Cache button under Settings or by clicking Sign Out.

3. Google API Services User Data Policy & Limited Use Compliance

Pulse's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs adheres strictly to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements:

  • Permitted Use Only: Data retrieved from Google Health APIs is used exclusively to render user-facing visualizations, charts, and activity tables inside your personal Pulse dashboard.
  • Zero Data Selling or Transfer: We never sell, rent, license, or transfer your Google user data to advertising networks, data brokers, or third-party analytics platforms.
  • No AI / ML Model Training: Your Google Health data is never used to train, fine-tune, or prompt generalized or personalized artificial intelligence or machine learning models.
  • No Human Access: Pulse personnel do not read or inspect your Google user data.

4. Exact OAuth Scopes Requested

Pulse requests read-only OAuth 2.0 scopes strictly necessary to display your health insights:

googlehealth.sleep.readonly — Visualizing sleep duration trends and sleep stages.
googlehealth.health_metrics_and_measurements.readonly — Visualizing Resting Heart Rate (RHR) and HRV trends.
googlehealth.activity_and_fitness.readonly — Visualizing daily step counts, active minutes, and active calories.
userinfo.email & userinfo.profile — Identifying your active dashboard session.

5. Instant Revocation & Cross-Account Protection (RISC)

You can revoke Pulse's access at any time from your Google Account Security Settings or by signing out inside the app. Additionally, Pulse implements Google Cross-Account Protection (RISC Security Event Tokens); if Google signals a security event or token revocation, Pulse immediately invalidates the active session.
