Privacy Policy

At Pulse, your personal health privacy is our highest priority. We engineered a 100% stateless architecture to ensure your sensitive Google Health metrics remain under your absolute control and are never stored in databases or shared with third parties.

100% Stateless SaaS

No databases, hard drives, or cloud data lakes. Your health metrics are processed entirely in temporary server memory.

Zero Data Selling

We never sell, rent, monetize, or share your Google user data with advertisers, data brokers, or AI training models.

Instant Revocation

Disconnecting from your dashboard or revoking permissions via your Google Account wipes all session tokens instantly.

1. Introduction & Overview

Welcome to Pulse ("we," "our," or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how our web application collects, uses, processes, and protects information when you connect your Google Account and access Google Health data (including data from Fitbit, Pixel Watch, and Google Fit integration endpoints).

By using Pulse, you consent to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not connect your Google Account or use the Pulse dashboard.

Core Commitment

Pulse is designed from the ground up as an on-the-fly visualizer. We do not want to store your personal health data, and our technical architecture physically prevents persistent data storage.

2. Google API Services User Data Policy Compliance

Pulse's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Limited Use Guarantee

We restrict our use of Google user data strictly to providing and improving real-time user-facing features within the Pulse dashboard. We do not transfer this data to third parties, use it for advertising, or allow human reading of user data except as required by law or with explicit affirmative user consent.

3. Google OAuth Scopes Requested & Explanation

When you initiate OAuth authentication with Google, Pulse requests explicit read-only permissions (scopes) to display your biometric and activity summaries. Below is the complete list of requested scopes and the exact justification for each:

OAuth Scope Data Type Why Pulse Needs This Scope
googlehealth.sleep.readonly Sleep Metrics Used to calculate sleep duration, efficiency percentage, REM/Deep/Light sleep stages, and sleep quality trends on your dashboard.
googlehealth.health_metrics_and_measurements.readonly Heart & Vitals Used to display resting heart rate (RHR), heart rate variability (HRV), and vital signs to assess cardiovascular recovery.
googlehealth.activity_and_fitness.readonly Activity & Energy Used to chart daily step counts, active energy burned (kcal), and physical workout duration.
googlehealth.profile.readonly Health Profile Used to read non-identifying physiological baselines (such as age range or height) required to accurately format metabolic calculations.
userinfo.email & userinfo.profile Identity Profile Used to display your first name, email address, and profile avatar in the dashboard navigation header during your active session.

4. Stateless Architecture & Data Storage

Unlike traditional health platforms that store biometric data in cloud databases, Pulse operates as a 100% Stateless Application. This provides an unprecedented level of security and privacy:

  • Volatile In-Memory Processing: When your dashboard loads or when you click "Live Refresh," Pulse sends an authenticated request to the Google Health API. The returning JSON data is parsed in server RAM solely to format the statistical charts delivered to your browser.
  • No Persistent Database: Pulse does not use SQL, NoSQL, or local file storage systems. No records of your heart rate, sleep cycles, steps, or identity are ever written to non-volatile disk storage.
  • Session Cookie Isolation: Your Google OAuth access and refresh tokens are encrypted and stored inside a temporary, HTTP-only browser session cookie. The server does not retain a master database of user tokens.
  • Automatic Data Purge: When your session terminates, the in-memory variables containing your health metrics are garbage-collected and permanently destroyed.

5. How We Use Your Data

Information accessed via Google Health APIs is used exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Real-Time Visualization: Rendering interactive graphs, summary cards, and time-horizon trends (e.g., 7-day or 14-day views) on your personal screen.
  • Session Authentication: Verifying your identity so that your browser displays the correct personal health metrics during your active login session.
  • System Reliability: Monitoring ephemeral server health and API response latency without logging personal user payloads.

We strictly prohibit using your Google Health data for profiling, automated decision-making, insurance underwriting, or eligibility determinations.

6. Third-Party Data Sharing & AI Disclaimer

We maintain a strict zero-tolerance policy regarding data dissemination:

  • No Sale or Renting: We do not sell, rent, lease, or trade your personal information or health metrics to any third party under any circumstances.
  • No Advertising Networks: We do not share Google user data with ad networks, data brokers, or marketing analytics platforms.
  • No Artificial Intelligence Training: Your health metrics, sleep patterns, heart rate records, and profile data are NEVER used to train, fine-tune, or validate artificial intelligence models, machine learning algorithms, or large language models (LLMs).
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information only if required by a valid subpoena, court order, or binding government demand, though our stateless architecture ensures we possess no archived health records to produce.

7. User Rights & Revoking Access

You maintain absolute control over your Google Account integration at all times:

  • In-App Disconnect: You can click the "Sign Out & Disconnect" button inside the Settings tab of the Pulse dashboard at any time. This immediately clears your browser session cookie and purges any active in-memory data views.
  • Google Account Security Revocation: You can permanently revoke Pulse's access permissions directly through your Google Account settings by visiting https://myaccount.google.com/connections and removing the Pulse application. Once revoked, Pulse can no longer query any Google APIs on your behalf.
